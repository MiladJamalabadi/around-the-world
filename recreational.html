1-Qeshm salt cave

Qeshm salt cave is the largest salt cave in the world and is 6600 meters long. This cave is located 90 km from Qeshm city and 2 km from the coast. So you can visit this 570-year-old cave at the same time as a beach trip. During the research, breathing from the air inside the cave can help improve respiratory diseases such as asthma. Fragile salt sculptures, rivers of salt and tiny bats are what you can expect. There is not enough light inside the cave, so to take a closer look at the body of the lanterns, it is enough to bring a powerful flashlight. The salts inside the cave have medicinal properties, don't be afraid after tasting them!

2-Bosphorus Strait

The Bosphorus strait is considered the border between Asia and Europe in Turkey, and it is a strait that connects the Black Sea to the Sea of Marmara. This gorge has very beautiful views of the coastal areas of Istanbul and the city skyline of Istanbul from this gorge is very spectacular and famous. This strait is not only a place where international commercial ships pass, but it is also very attractive for tourists. In the Bosphorus, you can have a completely different experience of sightseeing in Istanbul.

3-Maya Bay

Maya Bay is a beautiful and amazing bay on Koh Phi Phi Island in Thailand, which is part of Koh Phi Phi Archipelago and consists of 6 islands. This area of ​​Thailand is a true paradise on earth that has not yet been touched by human hands and remains very pristine. Where is the best fun and includes: swimming in clear and beautiful waters, building sand castles, diving in the water or diving with friends. The best time to travel to Maya Bay is during the peak season of travelers and tourists, that is, from November to April, when the Andaman Sea is calm and access to the bay is easy. The most popular activity in this area is going underwater and diving in the bay, which gives tourists the opportunity to see the beautiful and amazing animals and plants under the sea.
